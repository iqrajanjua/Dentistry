The Dental Dentistry and Dental Clinics is a global leader in oral science and dental and craniofacial research.


A $61 million renovation of the Dental Science Building—completed in 2016—modernized dental clinics and enhanced research facilities at the college. In 2017, the college also began sustained investments in research infrastructure to make full use of its facilities, including multiple grant support specialists, a director of science writing and editing, and the Division of Biostatistics and Computational Biology. These renovations, and investments in infrastructure, along with a focus on recruiting, hiring, and supporting new researchers has resulted in a record number of external grant funds, largely from the National Institutes of Health, and a record number of grant applications.

Dental Student Research Program


The University of Iowa College of Dentistry and Dental Clinics has the most accomplished student research program in the country.


1. Participation


With over 40 dental students participating in the program, approximately one-eighth of all dental students participate in any given semester. Around 40% of all dental students at Iowa spend at least one year participating in the program. Among all institutions in the National Student Research Group, Iowa had the greatest percentage of student members for 2018-2019.


2. Accomplishments


Graduates of the program learn how evidence and research makes a difference in all areas of dentistry—whether in private practice or in academic or clinical research.


3.Productivity


In 2023, dental student researchers had 8 publications, three of which with a dental student as the first author. For the 2022-2023 academic year, 27 students presented their research at national conferences (e.g., the IADR, the AADOCR, NOHC, AAPD, etc.).
