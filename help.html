wisdom Tooth

Wisdom teeth that erupt in an upright and functional position and do not cause pain often do not need to be extracted. However, impacted third molars can lead to a significant amount of pain and other serious dental problems, in which case wisdom tooth extraction is usually recommended. Wisdom teeth are often easier to remove before age 20, when they have less developed roots. So, do not wait to contact your periodontist if you or your teenaged son or daughter are experiencing wisdom tooth-related discomfort.

Healthy Tooth

Healthy teeth possess distinct characteristics, including strong enamel, uniform color, absence of stains or discoloration, smooth surface, proper shape and alignment, healthy gums, and freedom from sensitivity or pain Healthy teeth are supported by healthy gums. Gums should be firm, pink, and fit snugly around the teeth. Signs of gum disease, such as redness, swelling, bleeding, or receding gums, indicate an unhealthy condition. Regular brushing, flossing, and professional cleanings are essential for maintaining gum health..

Tooth Decay

Tooth decay is damage to a tooth's surface, or enamel. It happens when bacteria in your mouth make acids that attack the enamel. Tooth decay can lead to cavities (dental caries), which are holes in your teeth. If tooth decay is not treated, it can cause pain, infection, and even tooth loss.

Dental Care

Regular teeth cleaning by a dentist removes plaque that may develop, even with careful brushing and flossing. This is very important for getting at areas that are hard to reach on your own. Professional cleaning includes scaling and polishing. This procedure uses instruments to loosen and remove deposits from the teeth. Routine exams may include dental x-rays. Your dentist can catch problems early, so they do not become more serious and expensive to fix.
